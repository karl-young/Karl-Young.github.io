Te Whare Tapa Whā

Developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1984, Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model of health and wellbeing. It is a metaphor based on four pillars of a wharenui/meeting house. When we look after all four aspects, we look after our hauora/wellbeing. Checking in on the four pillars helps us balance our hauora and support others to balance theirs, too. Below are some examples for supporting you and your whānau.

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

Staying physically well is important. i will keep my physical wellbeing up by haveing a proper sleeping plan, meditating and focuing on breathing, going for walks at least 30mins and working out for 30mins every other day.

Taha Hinengaro (mental wellbeing)

Keeping your mind active. I will keep up my mental wellbeing by talking with my partner and meditation

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

Keeping your spirits high. I will keep my spiritual wellbeing up by listening to uplifting music, listen to the birds outside and soaking up the suns rays whenever i get a chance.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

Who makes you feel like you belong, who you care about and who you share your life with. I will keep up my family wellbeing by spending time with my family and friends on weekends and quick catchups during the week
